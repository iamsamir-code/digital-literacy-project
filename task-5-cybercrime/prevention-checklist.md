Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist For College Students in India

#General Online Safety

1.**Never share your OTP with anyone** — no bank, no company, 
no college authority will ever ask for it, not even once
2.**Use strong and unique passwords** — avoid using your name, 
date of birth or anything someone could guess easily. 
Use a mix of letters, numbers and symbols
3.**Enable two-factor authentication** on every important account 
Gmail, Instagram, LinkedIn, everything
4.**Don't click on unknown links** sent via WhatsApp, SMS or email 
even if the message looks official or urgent
5.**Check the URL before entering any login details** — fake websites 
look exactly like real ones but the URL is always slightly off
6.**Never use public WiFi for anything sensitive** — no banking, 
no logging into accounts, nothing important.

#UPI and Financial Safety 🏦

1.Approving a UPI collect request means YOU are paying — if someone says accept this request to receive money, that is a scam, close the app immediately
2.Never pay any upfront fee to receive a prize, scholarship or cashback— legitimate rewards don't work that way, ever

#If Something Goes Wrong

If you are a victim of any cybercrime or online fraud:

- 🌐 Report immediately at: cybercrime.gov.in
- 📞 Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930** (available 24x7)

The sooner you report, the higher the chance of action being taken. 
Don't feel embarrassed — these scams are designed by professionals 
to fool people. Reporting helps protect others too.
