Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud
What it is:
UPI fraud is basically when someone tricks you into sending them money through apps like PhonePe, Google Pay or Paytm — usually by making you think you're the one receiving money when you're actually the one paying.
How it happened — step by step:
Rahul was a first year engineering student who got a WhatsApp message one evening from an unknown number. The message said he'd been selected for a ₹10,000 merit scholarship from his college and just needed to verify his UPI ID to receive it.
The person on the other end sent him a UPI collect request for ₹10 and told him it was just a small verification amount that would come right back. Rahul saw the notification, assumed he was about to receive money, and approved it without actually reading what the request said.
Then came another collect request — this time for ₹5,000 — with the explanation that it was a one time processing fee that would be included in the final scholarship amount. Rahul approved that too. By the time he called his friend to share the good news, he'd already lost ₹5,010 with no way to get it back.
Who gets targeted:
Mostly first year students, elderly people, and anyone who hasn't used UPI long enough to know the difference between sending and receiving.
Consequences:
The money was gone instantly and permanently. No bank reversal, no refund, nothing. Beyond the financial loss Rahul felt embarrassed and anxious about using UPI for weeks after.
The one thing to remember:
If someone is genuinely sending you money on UPI, you don't need to do anything — it just arrives. Any situation where you have to approve or accept something to receive money is a scam. Every single time.
