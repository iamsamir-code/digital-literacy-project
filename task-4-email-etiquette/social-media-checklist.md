# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

#Do's
- **Think before you post** — ask yourself if you'd be okay with your professor or future employer seeing it
- **Use privacy settings** — not everything needs to be visible to everyone, control who sees your content
- **Give credit** — if you're sharing someone else's work, idea or photo, mention them
- **Keep your profile clean and professional** — LinkedIn especially, but even Instagram can be checked by recruiters
- **Verify before sharing** — if something sounds too shocking to be true, it probably isn't true

#Don'ts 

- **Don't post when angry** — venting publicly about college, professors or companies almost always backfires
- **Don't share personal information** — your address, phone number, daily location or financial details have no business being public
- **Don't spread unverified news** — forwarding something without checking it makes you part of the problem
- **Don't engage with online arguments** — especially on public posts, it never ends well and stays on the internet forever
- **Don't post exam or internal college content** — question papers, internal marks, confidential notices — none of this should go online
